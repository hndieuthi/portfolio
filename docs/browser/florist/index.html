Project overview

As the first project that was executed during Google UX Design course, I created Blooms - a florist app that help you buy flowers and customized them personally for your loved ones with just a few easy taps on the phone



TIMELINE

December 2021 - March 2022

WHAT I DID

Solo designer - user research, user flow, wireframe, hi-fi prototype, user testing, style guide


Research
User research summary
After conducting user interviews, there are some main takeaways to confirm the problems with buying flowers online

⏰ Time-consuming to find credible shops
It is easy to find a florist online, but very time-consuming if users dedicated to find a apposite option
🌷 Low durability
Flowers are not durable enough compared to other gift options

🛍 Shopping online made things less personal
Rating system for shop flowers are generally inadequate
📱 Technological barrier
Interviewed users complained about the usability experience they were having on other florist app.

“Went to Google and didn’t know which florists to pick”

“Can’t see how big the bouquet is”
“App is difficult to use”

“Overwhelmed with the web’s complex flower listing”

“Flowers easily wilt, I want the gifts to be last longer”

“Shopping online makes it less personal, lack of attention and care”

“Feel awful when I cannot be there to give the flowers myself”

“The voice transcription feature is hidden in the context menu, made it difficult to see”
Competitor analysis: Opportunity and gaps

I analyzed 3 competitors that allow users to buy flowers online, while keeping previous user research in mind. The gap became great opportunity for our solution

The gap
The competitors solely focus on functional features and have little customization for users to feel more involved in the process of giving flowers

Wireframes

After 2 rounds of usability studies, findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframe to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups need refining.

Notable improvements
1. E-greeting card visualization
Before

Originally, 3 kinds of greeting cards are made into buttons for users to choose. 3 out of 5 participants find it hard to imagine what the e-greeting card will be when sent to the recipient's email

After

The greeting card then is made exactly like what would be sent to the recipient’s email later. Users need to add cover and suitable gift message.

2. Omit Quick Check-out feature
Before

The Quick check-out function is added in the Search screen to quickly add and buy items without going into the Product detail screen. Participants found that it is confusing and rather take it slow.

The Quick check-out function is added in the Search screen to quickly add and buy items without going into the Product detail screen. Participants found that it is confusing and rather take it slow.

After

In order to make the flow more natural and simple, the Quick check-out function is omitted.
